About the book

During the slavery period in the history of the United States of America as a rising nation, a law in the state of South Carolina forbade any person of color (Negroes) to learn how to read. The fear that the slaves may gain wisdom and revolt against their masters was so vivid that a slave caught with the ability to read and write could be executed. Today, we count among people of color doctors, engineers, savants, etc. because wisdom and knowledge were made accessible.
